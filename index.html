Binaural Beat Generator

Background: Binaural beats are thought to boost activity in the brain through brainwave entrainment via the frequency-following response. To bias your state of consciousness towards a desired state of activity, select a frequency in the range corresponding to the brain wave state that you desire to experience.
Instructions: Use headphones for the best binaural beat experience. The left ear receives the carrier frequency minus half the beat frequency, and the right ear receives the carrier frequency plus half the beat frequency. Adjust the volume until the beat is barely perceptible, then allow it to fade into the background as you engage in some other activity.

The Binaural Beat Frequency Slider only goes up to 40 because most listeners stop perceiving the binaural beat effect around that point, but you can set it as high as you want using the number entry box. Values above double the Beat Carrier Frequency will clamp the left ear output to a minimum nonzero value.
Brain Wave Guide:

Delta Waves (0.5 - 4 Hz): Associated with deep, dreamless sleep, unconsciousness.

Theta Waves (4 - 8 Hz): Linked to deep meditation, REM sleep, and creativity, strong emotional connection.

Alpha Waves (8 - 13 Hz): Corresponds to a state of relaxed wakefulness and reduced anxiety, calm alertness, light meditation.

Beta Waves (13 - 30 Hz): Associated with active thinking, focus, concentration, and alertness. High-beta frequencies are associated with stress, excitement, or anxiety.

Gamma Waves (>40 Hz): Associated with peak concentration and focus, problem-solving and insight, high-level memory recall.

Suggestion: For studying, experiment with the range from 6 Hz mid-alpha up to 18 Hz mid-beta waves. Going lower may make you sleepy, and going higher may make you anxious, although 40 Hz is also a great frequency to try! Only by trying different frequencies will you find what works best for you.
